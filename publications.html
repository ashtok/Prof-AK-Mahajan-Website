Publications & Research

Explore Prof. Mahajan's extensive research contributions in geological sciences, spanning seismic hazard assessment, disaster management, and environmental sciences.

Research Focus Areas

Prof. Mahajan's research has primarily focused on understanding geological hazards and their mitigation strategies, with particular emphasis on seismic activity in the Himalayan region. His work combines field investigation, laboratory analysis, and computational modeling to develop comprehensive approaches to geological risk assessment and management.

Key research themes include:

  • Seismic microzonation and hazard assessment
  • Landslide susceptibility mapping and monitoring
  • Environmental impact of geological processes
  • GIS applications in disaster management
  • Hydrogeological studies in mountain environments

Journal Publications

Seismic Microzonation of Dharamshala Town, Himachal Pradesh (India): An Evaluation for Future Earthquakes

Mahajan, A.K., Thakur, V.C., Sharma, M.L. & Chauhan, M.
Journal of Seismology, 24(2), 331-349
2022
This study presents a comprehensive seismic microzonation framework for Dharamshala town, located in the seismically active Himalayan region. Using a multi-parameter approach incorporating geological, geomorphological, and geotechnical data, we developed hazard maps highlighting areas susceptible to amplified ground motion during seismic events. The results indicate substantial variation in seismic response across different geological formations, with implications for urban planning and disaster preparedness.

GIS-based Landslide Susceptibility Mapping and Assessment: A Case Study from the Northwest Himalaya, India

Mahajan, A.K., Singh, S., Kumar, N. & Chauhan, G.
Environmental Earth Sciences, 79(15), 1-18
2020
This paper presents a GIS-based methodology for landslide susceptibility mapping in the tectonically active Northwest Himalayan region. Utilizing analytical hierarchy process (AHP) and weighted overlay analysis, we integrated multiple causative factors including slope, aspect, lithology, drainage, and land use to generate comprehensive susceptibility maps. Field validation demonstrated high accuracy of the predictive model, providing a valuable tool for hazard mitigation and land-use planning in mountainous regions prone to landslides.

Hydrogeological Characterization and Groundwater Potential Assessment in Mountainous Terrain of Himachal Pradesh, India

Mahajan, A.K., Sharma, R.K. & Kumar, P.
Hydrogeology Journal, 28(4), 1295-1311
2019
This research investigates groundwater dynamics in the complex hydrogeological setting of the Himalayan mountains. Through integrated analysis of geological structures, lithological variations, and hydrological parameters, we identified potential groundwater zones and developed a conceptual model for groundwater flow in folded and faulted terrain. The study provides insights into sustainable groundwater management strategies for mountainous regions facing increasing water scarcity due to climate change and growing anthropogenic pressures.

Assessment of Environmental Impacts of Limestone Mining in the Lesser Himalaya: A Case Study from Himachal Pradesh, India

Mahajan, A.K., Gupta, V. & Das, A.
Environmental Monitoring and Assessment, 191(2), 71
2018
This study evaluates the environmental consequences of limestone mining activities in the ecologically sensitive Lesser Himalayan region. Through multi-temporal satellite imagery analysis and field-based environmental quality assessments, we documented significant landscape alterations, habitat fragmentation, and deterioration of air and water quality in mining-affected areas. The research highlights the need for stringent environmental regulations and restoration strategies to mitigate the adverse impacts of mining on fragile mountain ecosystems.

Supervised Research

2022

Integrated Approach for Landslide Hazard Assessment and Mapping in the Northwest Himalaya

Student: Dr. Neelam Sharma

This dissertation developed a multi-criteria decision analysis framework for comprehensive landslide hazard assessment in the tectonically active Himalayan terrain. Combining remote sensing, GIS techniques, and field investigations, the research produced high-resolution hazard maps that achieved over 85% prediction accuracy when validated against historical landslide inventories. The methodology has been adopted by regional disaster management authorities for landslide risk reduction planning.

2020

Seismic Response Analysis and Microzonation of Urban Areas in the Himalayan Foothills

Student: Dr. Rohit Kumar

This research conducted a detailed investigation of site-specific seismic response characteristics in rapidly urbanizing areas of the Himalayan foothills. Using microtremor measurements, shallow geophysical surveys, and geotechnical testing, the study identified areas susceptible to seismic amplification and developed microzonation maps for urban planning. The dissertation included recommendations for building codes tailored to local geological conditions to enhance earthquake resilience.

2018

Hydrogeological Investigation and Groundwater Quality Assessment in Tectonically Active Mountain Watersheds

Student: Dr. Priyanka Sharma

This dissertation examined the complex hydrogeological systems in the Himalayan region, focusing on groundwater quality, recharge mechanisms, and vulnerability to contamination. Employing isotopic tracers, hydrochemical analysis, and numerical modeling, the research elucidated groundwater flow paths and residence times in fractured aquifer systems. The findings contributed to the development of watershed management strategies for sustainable groundwater utilization in mountain environments.

2023

Assessment of Flash Flood Hazards in Mountain Streams Using Geomorphological Indicators

Student: Vikram Singh

This study developed a methodology for evaluating flash flood susceptibility in mountain watersheds based on geomorphological parameters and precipitation patterns. Field mapping, hydrological analysis, and historical records were integrated to categorize stream reaches according to flood hazard levels. The research produced actionable guidelines for local authorities to prioritize flood mitigation measures.

2022

Mapping and Monitoring of Active Faults Using Remote Sensing and Geomorphic Analysis

Student: Anisha Gupta

This dissertation employed high-resolution satellite imagery and digital elevation models to identify and characterize active faults in the seismically dynamic Himalayan region. Geomorphic indices including mountain front sinuosity, valley floor width-to-height ratio, and stream length gradient were calculated to assess relative tectonic activity. The research contributed to enhanced understanding of earthquake potential in poorly studied fault segments.

2021

Evaluation of Climate Change Impacts on Glacial Lake Outburst Flood Hazards

Student: Ravi Kant

This study investigated the increasing risk of glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs) in the Himalayan region due to accelerated glacial retreat. Multi-temporal satellite imagery analysis documented lake expansion trends, while field surveys and dam stability assessments identified potentially dangerous glacial lakes. The research produced hazard maps highlighting vulnerable downstream communities and infrastructure, contributing to early warning system development.

Notable Projects

Multi-Hazard Risk Assessment for the Himalayan Region

2020-2023
Funded by: National Disaster Management Authority

A comprehensive evaluation of multiple geological hazards including earthquakes, landslides, and flash floods to develop integrated risk assessment methodologies. The project produced hazard maps and vulnerability assessments for five districts, directly informing disaster management planning at regional and local levels.

Seismic Microzonation of Urban Centers in the Northwestern Himalaya

2018-2021
Funded by: Ministry of Earth Sciences

Detailed characterization of seismic hazards in rapidly growing urban areas through geophysical surveys, geotechnical investigations, and ground motion modeling. The project delivered site-specific seismic amplification maps that have been incorporated into urban development plans and building regulations.

Climate Change Impacts on Mountain Hydrology

2016-2019
Funded by: Department of Science and Technology

Investigation of changing hydrological regimes in Himalayan watersheds under climate change scenarios. The project utilized field monitoring, isotopic tracing, and hydrological modeling to predict shifts in water availability, affecting agriculture, hydropower, and domestic water supply. Policy recommendations were developed for adaptive water management strategies.

Landslide Early Warning System Development

2015-2018
Funded by: International Cooperation Program

A collaborative international project to develop and implement a community-based landslide early warning system in landslide-prone regions. The project combined technical monitoring equipment with local knowledge networks to create effective warning communication channels, resulting in successful evacuation during multiple landslide events.
